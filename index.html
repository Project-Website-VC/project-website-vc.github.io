About

I am a faculty member working at the intersection of information, data, and society. My work focuses on trustworthy AI, conversational systems, and human-computer interaction, with an emphasis on ethical governance and equitable outcomes.

  • Ethical AI
  • Conversational Agents
  • Human-AI Interaction

Research

Current interests include the bidirectional nature of bias in LLMs, trustworthy conversational systems, and practical, human-centered evaluation methods.

  • Bias & Evaluation: Methods to surface and mitigate reciprocal bias in large language models.
  • Conversational IR: Designing explainable, dialog-centric retrieval experiences.
  • Community Impact: Applying AI responsibly in libraries, archives, and public services.
